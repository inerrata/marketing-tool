## Content pillars

Pillars are 3–5 recurring themes that everything you publish ladders up to. They sit at the intersection
of three things: what your audience cares about, what you have authority/credibility on, and what
supports your business goals.

**To define pillars:**
1. List the audience's top problems, questions, and aspirations.
2. List your areas of genuine expertise / unique point of view.
3. List the topics that, if the audience associated them with you, would drive business.
4. The overlaps become your pillars. Name each clearly.

Good pillars are broad enough to sustain dozens of pieces but specific enough to build a reputation.
"Marketing" is too broad; "B2B email that doesn't feel like spam" is a pillar.

## Funnel mapping

Map content to where the audience is in their journey. A library skewed entirely to one stage underperforms.

- **TOFU — Awareness** (they have a problem, don't know you): educational how-tos, opinion/POV pieces,
  data studies, trend pieces, entertaining/relatable content. Goal: reach + trust. Metric: reach,
  new followers, organic traffic.
- **MOFU — Consideration** (evaluating options): comparisons, case studies, webinars, deep guides,
  frameworks, email nurture. Goal: educate + build preference. Metric: email signups, content downloads,
  return visits.
- **BOFU — Decision** (ready to choose): testimonials, demos, ROI tools, free trials, pricing clarity,
  objection-handling content. Goal: convert. Metric: trials, demos booked, sales.

A healthy program feeds all three, weighted toward TOFU/MOFU for reach with enough BOFU to capture demand.

## Content calendar

**Structure each entry with:**
- Date / publish slot
- Platform / channel
- Pillar
- Funnel stage
- Format (post, article, video, email, etc.)
- Working title / hook
- Primary CTA
- Goal metric
- Status (idea / drafting / scheduled / live)
- Owner (if a team)

**Cadence principles:**
- Consistency beats volume. A sustainable 2x/week beats an unsustainable daily burst that fizzles.
- Batch creation, schedule distribution. Produce in focused blocks; publish on a steady drip.
- Leave room (~20%) for timely/reactive content — news, trends, audience questions.
- Plan around key business moments (launches, events, seasons) first, then fill the rest.

**Default planning horizon:** a themed month (each week or fortnight anchored to a pillar or campaign) with
the next 2–4 weeks fully drafted and the rest at the idea/title stage.

## Content mix & ratios

A workable default ratio (adjust per brand and channel):
- **~60% educational / value** — builds trust and reach; the bulk of what you publish.
- **~25% brand / social proof / story** — case studies, behind-the-scenes, culture, wins.
- **~15% promotional / direct CTA** — offers, launches, direct asks.

If everything is promotional, the audience tunes out. If nothing is, you don't convert. The value-heavy
ratio earns the right to occasionally sell.

## Formats by goal

- **Reach/awareness:** short-form video, opinion posts, data/original research, listicles, memes (if on-brand).
- **Authority:** deep guides, frameworks, original research, expert interviews, teardowns.
- **Trust/proof:** case studies, testimonials, transparent build-in-public, comparisons.
- **Conversion:** demos, free tools/templates, webinars, email sequences, BOFU comparison content.
- **Retention/community:** newsletters, user spotlights, tutorials, Q&A.

Match format to goal rather than chasing whatever format is trendy.

## Repurposing

One substantial piece should fuel many. This is the highest-leverage habit in content.

**A "pillar → atomized" flow:**
1. Create one substantial asset (a deep guide, a research piece, a long video, a webinar).
2. Extract: key takeaways → individual social posts; the framework → a carousel/infographic; a quote →
   a graphic; a section → a newsletter; the video → clips; the data → a chart post.
3. Distribute across channels over weeks, each adapted to the platform's native format.

Plan repurposing at creation time, not as an afterthought — produce the pillar with the atoms in mind.

## Distribution

Creation is half the job; distribution is the other half. A common failure is publishing and hoping.

- **Owned:** your site, email list, communities you run. Highest control, build relentlessly (esp. email).
- **Earned:** shares, mentions, PR, word of mouth. Built through quality + relationships.
- **Paid:** ads to amplify proven organic content. Amplify winners; don't promote duds.

Rule of thumb: spend at least as much effort distributing a piece as creating it. Reshare, repurpose,
email it, seed it in communities, turn it into other formats.

## Content audit

When auditing existing content:
1. **Inventory** — list all content with: URL/location, topic, pillar, format, date, current performance.
2. **Assess each piece** against: still accurate? still on-strategy? performing? aligned to a pillar and
   funnel stage?
3. **Decide per piece:** keep, update/refresh, consolidate (merge thin pieces), repurpose, or retire.
4. **Find gaps** — pillars or funnel stages that are underserved, audience questions with no content.
5. **Prioritize** — refreshing a high-potential underperformer often beats creating net-new.

Output a prioritized action list, not just observations.
