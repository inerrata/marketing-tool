## Campaign brief

Every campaign starts with a one-page brief. It forces clarity and aligns everyone. Never skip it.

**The brief (one page, no more):**
- **Objective** — one measurable goal. "Drive 500 trial signups in 6 weeks," not "raise awareness."
- **Audience** — who, and critically: what do they believe/feel NOW, and what do we want them to
  believe/feel AFTER? This before/after shift is the heart of the campaign.
- **Single key message** — the one thing the audience must take away. If you have three, you have none.
- **Reason to believe** — the proof that makes the message credible.
- **Call to action** — the specific action we want.
- **Channels** — where we'll show up (chosen based on where the audience is).
- **Timeline** — start, key milestones, end.
- **Budget tier** — if known, even roughly (shapes channel and creative choices).
- **Success metrics** — primary (tied to objective) + secondary (leading indicators).

## Campaign concept development

The concept is the creative idea that carries the message — the hook, angle, or through-line that makes
the campaign memorable and coherent across assets.

**To develop a concept:**
1. Start from the audience's before→after shift and the single key message.
2. Find the emotional truth — what does the audience feel about this problem? (frustration, fear, FOMO,
   aspiration, relief, pride).
3. Generate angles: problem-dramatization, surprising reframe, bold promise, relatable story, contrarian
   take, demonstration, social proof, transformation.
4. Pick the angle that's both true to the product and emotionally resonant.
5. Express it as a through-line that can flex across every asset (a phrase, a visual motif, a recurring
   format) — so the email, the landing page, the ads, and the social posts feel like one campaign.

A strong concept is ownable, repeatable across assets, and tied to a real product truth (not a gimmick
bolted on top).

## Product launch

**Phased launch structure:**

**1. Pre-launch (build anticipation)**
- Tease the problem you solve; collect a waitlist/interest list.
- Seed with early users, beta feedback, testimonials to have proof ready at launch.
- Prepare all assets in advance (page, emails, ads, social, PR).

**2. Launch (concentrate attention)**
- Coordinate a burst: announcement across all owned channels same day, email to list, social, any PR/
  partnerships timed together. Concentrated attention beats a slow trickle.
- Make the offer and CTA crystal clear. Often a launch incentive (early-bird pricing, bonus, deadline).
- Be present to respond — launch day is high-engagement.

**3. Post-launch (sustain & convert)**
- Follow-up email sequence to non-converters (more proof, objection handling, deadline).
- Share momentum proof ("300 signups in 48 hours," user reactions).
- Capture and publish early-customer testimonials.
- Retarget engaged-but-didn't-convert audiences.
- Debrief: what worked, what to keep for next time.

**Launch asset checklist:** landing/launch page, announcement email + follow-up sequence, social posts
(teaser/launch/proof), paid creative (if applicable), PR/outreach list, internal/partner enablement,
FAQ/objection doc, success dashboard.

## Go-to-market (GTM)

Broader than a single launch — how you bring an offer to a market and acquire customers repeatably.

**GTM components:**
1. **Target market & ICP** — who exactly, segmented (see research.md).
2. **Positioning & messaging** — why you, why now (see brand-messaging.md).
3. **Offer & pricing** — what you sell and how it's packaged/priced.
4. **Channels** — the routes to your audience (content, paid, outbound, partnerships, community,
   product-led). Choose based on where the audience is and unit economics.
5. **Acquisition motion** — the repeatable path from stranger → customer (e.g. content → email → trial →
   sale; or outbound → demo → close; or product-led free → paid).
6. **Funnel & conversion points** — the stages and what moves someone between them.
7. **Metrics & targets** — CAC, conversion rates per stage, payback, target volumes (see measurement.md).
8. **Sequencing** — what you do first (usually: nail one channel and one motion before adding more).

**GTM principle:** focus beats breadth early. One sharp ICP, one channel, one motion, executed well, beats
spreading thin across many. Expand once you have a working motion.

## Asset planning

For any campaign, map the assets needed before producing them:
- **Hero asset** — the centerpiece (launch page, flagship video, signature guide).
- **Acquisition assets** — what brings people in (ads, social, PR, SEO content).
- **Conversion assets** — what closes (email sequence, demo, sales page, offer).
- **Proof assets** — what builds trust (testimonials, case studies, data).
- **Enablement assets** — internal/partner (sales one-pager, FAQ, talking points).

For each asset note: purpose, channel, owner, deadline, dependency. Sequence so dependencies (e.g. proof
needed before the page) are met.

## Timeline & sequencing

- Work backward from the launch/end date.
- Identify dependencies (page needs copy needs positioning; ads need creative needs concept).
- Build in buffer and a review checkpoint before go-live.
- Front-load anything that needs lead time (PR, partnerships, production).
- Define a clear "go" checklist: everything that must be true before launch (assets live, tracking working,
  team briefed, follow-up scheduled).
