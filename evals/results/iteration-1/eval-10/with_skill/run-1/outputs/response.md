# Campaign Brief: Free-Trial Signup Push — Time-Tracking Software for Freelance Designers

**Assumptions:** The software is a standalone time-tracking tool (not bundled in a larger suite); the free trial is self-serve (no sales call required); the primary competitor is either not tracking time at all or using a basic spreadsheet/timer. Adjust any of these if wrong.

---

## Campaign Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Campaign name** | "Every Hour Counts" |
| **Duration** | 6 weeks |
| **Primary goal** | Drive free-trial signups from freelance designers |
| **Success metric** | Number of trial activations (primary); trial-to-paid conversion rate week 6 (secondary) |
| **Budget tier** | TBD — brief works at any spend level; channel mix scales accordingly |

---

## Target Audience

**Who:** Freelance graphic, UI/UX, and brand designers — solo operators billing by the hour or project, managing 3–8 clients simultaneously.

**Their situation:** They undercharge because they don't know where their hours actually go. They suspect they're losing 5–10 hours a month to unbilled work but can't prove it. They've tried tracking time in a notes app or spreadsheet and quit within a week because it was too much friction.

**What they believe right now:** "I'm too busy to add another tool" and "I already know roughly how long things take." Both beliefs cost them money.

**What needs to shift:** They need to see that the pain of not tracking (lost revenue, client disputes, chronic underpricing) is larger than the pain of tracking.

---

## Single Campaign Message

> **Stop guessing. Start billing what you're actually worth.**

Every creative element — ad, email subject line, landing page headline — ladders back to this one idea: freelance designers lose real money by not knowing where their hours go, and this tool closes that gap in under 2 minutes a day.

---

## Offer

- Free trial (no credit card required — lead with this)
- Duration: [X days — fill in your trial length]
- Friction: self-serve signup, under 60 seconds

---

## Channel Plan

| Channel | Role | Weeks |
|---|---|---|
| Paid social (Instagram, Pinterest, LinkedIn) | Acquisition — reach designers where they already are | 1–6 |
| Design community seeding (Dribbble, Behance forums, Designer Slack groups) | Organic trial seeding + social proof | 1–3 |
| Email to existing free/inactive users | Re-engagement + referral prompt | Week 1 and Week 4 |
| Retargeting ads | Convert site visitors who didn't sign up | 2–6 |
| Influencer / creator partnership (1–2 freelance design YouTubers or newsletter writers) | Authentic demo + trust signal | Week 2 or 3 |

---

## Creative Direction

**Tone:** Direct, slightly wry, peer-to-peer. Speak like a fellow freelancer who's figured something out — not like a software company. Avoid words like "seamless," "powerful," or "robust."

**Visual language:** Real work environments — a designer's desk, an open Figma file, a client invoice. Not stock-photo-generic. Prioritize authenticity over polish.

**Headline angles to test (pick 2 for A/B):**
1. *Direct benefit:* "Find out how many hours you gave away free last month."
2. *Curiosity/loss:* "Your last project probably took 4 hours longer than you charged for."
3. *Social proof:* "Freelance designers using [Product] bill an average of [X]% more in their first month." *(Use only if you have this data.)*

---

## 6-Week Timeline

| Week | Focus | Key Actions |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Launch + seed | Go live with paid social; post organically in 3–5 design communities; send re-engagement email to lapsed users |
| **2** | Amplify | Launch retargeting; activate influencer/creator partnership; monitor which ad creative is pulling signups |
| **3** | Optimize | Cut underperforming ad variants; double budget on top creative; publish any earned social proof (screenshots, testimonials) |
| **4** | Mid-campaign push | Send second email to non-openers from Week 1; test a new headline angle on paid social |
| **5** | Urgency layer | Add a time-limited trial extension offer (e.g., "Start your trial this week — get 7 extra days free") to ad and email copy |
| **6** | Close + capture | Retarget all trial clickers who haven't signed up; send final email to warm list; capture learnings for next campaign |

## KPIs

| Metric | Tracked by | Review cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Free-trial signups (volume) | Product analytics / UTM tracking | Daily |
| Cost per trial signup (paid channels) | Ad platform dashboards | Weekly |
| Trial activation rate (signed up → used the product) | Product analytics | Weekly |
| Trial-to-paid conversion (week 6 read) | CRM / billing system | End of campaign |
| Top-performing creative/channel | UTM + ad platform | Weekly |

---

## What This Brief Does Not Cover

To execute this campaign you will also need: finalized ad creative, a dedicated landing page (or optimized trial signup page), UTM parameters for every channel, and a short onboarding email sequence to convert trialists. Each of these warrants its own brief or spec.
