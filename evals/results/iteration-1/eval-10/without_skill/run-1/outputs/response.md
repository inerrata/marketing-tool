# Campaign Brief: Free Trial Signup Push
**Product:** Time-Tracking Software
**Audience:** Freelance Designers
**Duration:** 6 Weeks
**Goal:** Drive Free Trial Signups

---

## Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** "Track It. Bill It. Own It."

**Objective:** Generate free trial signups from freelance designers by highlighting how the software eliminates the frustration of untracked hours, underbilling, and client disputes.

**Target Metric:** 1,500 new free trial signups over 6 weeks (benchmark against current baseline; adjust weekly).

---

## Target Audience

**Primary Persona: The Independent Designer**
- Freelance graphic, UX/UI, or brand designers working solo or in small collabs
- Charges clients hourly or project-based; juggles multiple clients simultaneously
- Pain points: forgetting to log hours, undercharging, struggling to justify invoices, context-switching between client work and admin
- Watering holes: Dribbble, Behance, Designer News, Reddit (r/freelance, r/graphic_design), Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube design channels
- Influenced by: peer recommendations, software that feels visually clean and minimal

---

## Core Messaging

**Value Proposition:** Stop leaving money on the table. Our time tracker is built for designers — beautiful, distraction-free, and synced with how creative work actually flows.

**Key Messages:**
1. "You design for a living. Start getting paid for every minute of it."
2. "No more guessing. Know exactly what to bill — and have the receipts."
3. "Start free. See the difference in your first invoice."

**Tone:** Empowering, peer-level, visually-aware. Avoid corporate jargon. Speak like a fellow creative who figured it out.

---

## Campaign Structure (6-Week Timeline)

### Week 1–2: Awareness & Resonance
- Launch organic social content on Instagram and LinkedIn with relatable "underbilling" scenarios (carousel posts, short-form video)
- Publish one SEO blog post: "How Freelance Designers Are Losing $500/Month Without Knowing It"
- Begin paid social targeting: Instagram + LinkedIn, interest-based (freelance design, Figma, Adobe Creative Suite)

### Week 3–4: Consideration & Proof
- Release a short demo video (60–90 sec): "A Day in the Life — With and Without Time Tracking"
- Email outreach to any existing waitlist or newsletter subscribers
- Partner with 2–3 mid-tier design influencers or newsletter writers for sponsored posts or honest reviews
- Retargeting ads to blog post visitors and social engagers

### Week 5–6: Conversion Push
- Launch limited-time incentive: "Start your free trial this month — get 3 months of premium free if you convert"
- Final push email sequence (2–3 emails) to warm leads with testimonials and objection handling
- Retargeting hard close ads: "Still thinking about it? Your first invoice tells the story."

## Creative Requirements

- 3–5 static ad creatives (clean, design-forward aesthetic — this audience will notice bad design)
- 1 short demo/explainer video
- 2–3 carousel posts for Instagram/LinkedIn
- 1 landing page variant optimized for free trial CTA (headline: "Built for Freelancers. Free to Start.")
- Email sequence: 3 emails (intro, proof/value, close)

- An existing landing page or ability to create a dedicated trial page is available
- Email list exists (even if small) to seed nurture sequence
- Product has a clean, modern UI appropriate for a design-conscious audience
