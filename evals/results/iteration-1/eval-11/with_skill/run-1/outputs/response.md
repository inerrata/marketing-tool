# ICP for Your Invoicing Tool — A Few Questions First

An ICP is load-bearing strategy. Everything downstream — your positioning, messaging, channel mix, pricing, content — inherits whatever assumptions go into it. A plausible-looking ICP built on guesses looks authoritative but sends you in the wrong direction.

Before I build it out, three sharp questions so it reflects your actual best customers rather than the average of everyone who could theoretically use invoicing software:

---

**1. Who is your current best customer — or who do you most want to target?**

Be as narrow as you can. For example:
- "Freelance designers and copywriters, solo, $50–150K/year"
- "Agencies of 2–10 people, services-based, no full-time bookkeeper"
- "US-based contractors in construction/trades"
- "SaaS founders who invoice enterprise clients on retainer"

If you already have customers, who among them gets the most value, complains the least, and pays on time? That's your ICP seed.

---

**2. What's the single most important pain this tool solves — and how does it solve it differently than the alternatives?**

Invoicing is a crowded category (FreshBooks, Wave, QuickBooks, plain PDF templates). What makes someone choose your tool specifically? For instance:
- Speed ("invoice created in under 60 seconds")
- Automation ("auto-reminders so you don't chase clients")
- Simplicity ("no accounting background needed")
- A specific workflow ("integrates with [specific tool] that others don't")
- Price ("free tier that actually works")

---

**3. What's the main alternative your best customer is coming from — or would default to if your tool didn't exist?**

Are they switching from a spreadsheet, from a competitor like Invoice Ninja or FreshBooks, or from doing nothing formal at all? The answer shapes the ICP's "trigger moment" (when they're most likely to switch) and the language that resonates.

---

Once I have these, I'll build you a complete ICP covering:
- Firmographic / demographic profile
- Psychographic and behavioral attributes
- Trigger events (what prompts them to look for a solution)
- Jobs-to-be-done
- Objections and how to address them
- Where to reach them (channels)
- Disqualifiers (who is NOT your ICP)

Happy to iterate from your answers — even rough notes are enough to work with.
