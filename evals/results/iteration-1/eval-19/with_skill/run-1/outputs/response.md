# What to Track for a New Paid Ads Channel

Here's a structured set of metrics to put in place from day one, organized by what they tell you and when they matter.

---

## Phase 1 — Delivery and efficiency (track immediately)

These tell you whether the channel is working mechanically before you worry about downstream results.

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Impressions** | Are ads being served? Is targeting too narrow? |
| **Click-through rate (CTR)** | Are ads compelling enough to earn a click? |
| **Cost per click (CPC)** | How efficiently are you buying attention? |
| **Spend vs. budget** | Is the channel consuming budget as planned? |

Baseline: compare CTR and CPC against published industry benchmarks for the channel and vertical — they vary widely (search vs. social vs. display).

---

## Phase 2 — Acquisition (track from week 1)

These connect ad spend to actual results.

| Metric | Formula | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost per lead (CPL)** | Ad spend ÷ leads generated | What you pay to get a hand-raiser |
| **Conversion rate** | Conversions ÷ clicks × 100 | How well the landing page + offer converts ad traffic |
| **Cost per acquisition (CAC)** | Ad spend ÷ new customers | The real cost of acquiring a paying customer |
| **ROAS** | Revenue from ads ÷ ad spend | Every dollar in, X dollars back |

ROAS is the headline number for most e-commerce. CAC is the headline for subscription or high-consideration products.

---

## Phase 3 — Channel health and efficiency over time

Once you have enough data (typically 4–8 weeks):

- **LTV:CAC ratio** — is the channel acquiring customers worth keeping? A rough benchmark is 3:1, but this varies by industry.
- **CAC payback period** = CAC ÷ monthly gross margin per customer. How many months until you recoup acquisition cost?
- **Frequency / ad fatigue** — rising CPCs or falling CTR often signal the audience has seen your ads too many times.
- **Impression share / auction metrics** (where the channel offers them) — are you losing to competitors on bid or quality?

---

## One primary metric per phase

Don't optimize everything at once. Pick one primary metric per stage:

1. **Ramp-up:** CTR — are ads resonating?
2. **Conversion focus:** CPL or CAC — are we getting results at the right cost?
3. **Scale decision:** ROAS or LTV:CAC — is this channel worth investing more?

---

## Attribution — set this up before you launch

Decide upfront how you'll assign credit:
- Use **UTM parameters** on every ad URL so you can trace channel, campaign, and ad-level traffic in analytics.
- Pick an attribution model (last-touch is the default in most platforms but overstates bottom-of-funnel; position-based or time-decay tends to be fairer if you run multi-channel).
- Add a **"how did you hear about us?"** field on your post-conversion form. Self-reported data catches what tracking misses.

---

## What not to get distracted by

- **Impressions alone** — reach without action is noise.
- **Platform-reported conversions without cross-checking** — ad platforms typically over-count using view-through attribution. Verify against your own analytics.
- **Early ROAS numbers** on subscription products — customers acquired in month 1 may not reflect full LTV for months.

---

## Quick-start checklist

- [ ] UTM parameters on all ad URLs
- [ ] Conversion tracking pixel/event firing correctly (test before spending)
- [ ] Baseline CPL or CAC target set (work backwards from acceptable LTV)
- [ ] Weekly cadence to review spend, CTR, CPL, and conversion rate
- [ ] Attribution model documented and agreed on
