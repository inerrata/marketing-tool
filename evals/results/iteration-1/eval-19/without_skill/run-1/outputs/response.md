# What to Track for a New Paid Ads Channel

When launching a new paid ads channel, you need to track metrics across three layers: **spend efficiency**, **funnel performance**, and **business impact**. Here's a structured breakdown.

---

## 1. Spend & Efficiency Metrics

These tell you if you're getting value for your money.

- **Cost Per Click (CPC)** — how much each click costs
- **Cost Per Mille (CPM)** — cost per 1,000 impressions (useful for awareness campaigns)
- **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)** — total spend divided by conversions; your core efficiency metric
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** — revenue generated per dollar spent (e.g., $4 ROAS = $4 revenue per $1 spent)
- **Budget pacing** — are you spending your budget evenly, or burning it too fast/slow?

---

## 2. Awareness & Reach Metrics

These measure how broadly your ads are reaching people.

- **Impressions** — total times your ad was shown
- **Reach** — unique people who saw it
- **Frequency** — average times each person saw your ad (high frequency = ad fatigue risk)
- **Share of Voice / Impression Share** (if available on the platform) — your presence vs. competitors

---

## 3. Engagement Metrics

These tell you if your creative and targeting are resonating.

- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** — clicks / impressions; benchmark varies by channel (search ~3-5%, social ~0.5-2%)
- **Engagement rate** — likes, shares, comments (relevant for social paid channels)
- **Video view rate / completion rate** — if running video ads
- **Ad Quality Score / Relevance Score** — platform-assigned scores (Google Quality Score, Meta Relevance Score) that affect your cost and delivery

---

## 4. Conversion Funnel Metrics

These track what happens after the click.

- **Landing page conversion rate** — % of visitors who take the desired action
- **Lead volume** — total form fills, sign-ups, or leads generated
- **Sales Qualified Leads (SQLs)** — if there's a sales team downstream
- **Add-to-cart / checkout rate** — for ecommerce
- **Micro-conversions** — email captures, content downloads, demo requests (useful early signals before you have enough purchase data)

---

## 5. Revenue & Business Impact Metrics

These tie ads back to real business outcomes.

- **Revenue attributed to the channel** — use UTM parameters and your analytics platform to track this
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** — total channel cost / new customers acquired
- **Lifetime Value to CAC ratio (LTV:CAC)** — ideally 3:1 or better
- **Payback period** — how many months until you recoup the CAC
- **New vs. returning customer split** — is the channel acquiring genuinely new customers?

---

## 6. Attribution & Data Quality

Often overlooked but critical.

- **UTM parameter setup** — make sure every ad has proper UTM source/medium/campaign/content tags
- **Conversion tracking verification** — confirm your pixel/tag is firing correctly before spending at scale
- **View-through vs. click-through conversions** — understand what the platform is claiming credit for
- **Cross-channel overlap** — watch for the new channel cannibalizing credit from existing channels in your attribution model

---

## 7. Benchmarks to Establish in Week 1–4

Before you can optimize, you need baselines:

| Metric | What to document |
|--------|-----------------|
| CPC | Starting cost per click by campaign/ad group |
| CPA | Initial cost per acquisition |
| CTR | Click-through rate by creative variant |
| Conversion rate | Landing page CR from paid traffic specifically |
| ROAS | Early revenue signal (even if data is thin) |

---

## Recommended Tracking Cadence

- **Daily**: Spend pacing, CPC, CTR, any anomalies
- **Weekly**: CPA, ROAS, conversion rate, lead volume
- **Monthly**: CAC, LTV:CAC, channel contribution to overall revenue, creative performance
- **Quarterly**: Full channel ROI review, payback period, budget reallocation decisions

---

## Tools to Set Up Before Launch

- UTM builder (e.g., Google's Campaign URL Builder)
- Google Analytics 4 or your existing analytics platform with goals configured
- Platform-native conversion tracking (Google Ads tag, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, etc.)
- A simple dashboard (Looker Studio, Databox, or even a spreadsheet) to centralize metrics across platforms

---

**The most important metric depends on your goal**: if you're focused on revenue, ROAS and CPA are primary. If you're in a growth/awareness phase, CPM, reach, and CTR matter more. Define your north star metric before launch so you're optimizing toward the right thing.
