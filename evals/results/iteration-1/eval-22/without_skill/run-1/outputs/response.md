# How to Get Your First 100 Customers

Getting your first 100 customers is a specific challenge — and the good news is it doesn't require a big ad budget. It requires hustle, directness, and targeting a small audience very well. Here's a practical playbook.

---

## 1. Tell Everyone You Know (Yes, Everyone)

Your personal network is your fastest first channel.

- Message friends, family, former coworkers, and acquaintances directly. Not a mass email — personal, individual messages.
- Tell them what you sell, why it's good, and ask them to either buy or share it with one person who might need it.
- This feels awkward. Do it anyway. Most first customers come from warm networks.

**Goal:** Get your first 10–20 customers this way.

---

## 2. Post in Communities Where Your Buyers Already Hang Out

Find the places your target customer spends time online.

- Reddit: Find subreddits related to your product category. Read the rules. Contribute genuinely before posting. Many subreddits allow "shameless self-promotion" threads.
- Facebook Groups: Search for groups relevant to your niche. Join and participate before pitching.
- Discord servers, Slack communities, forums — same approach.
- LinkedIn: If your product is B2B, post about the problem you solve and mention your store in the comments.

Do not spam. Be human. Add value first.

**Goal:** 20–40 more customers from community channels.

---

## 3. Create Content That Attracts the Right People

You don't need to go viral. You need to reach the right 100 people.

- **Short-form video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts):** Show the product in use, behind the scenes, or explain the problem it solves. Raw, authentic content outperforms polished ads at this stage.
- **Pinterest:** If your product is visual (home goods, fashion, food, crafts), Pinterest drives purchase intent and has long shelf life.
- **Blog or SEO:** Write one or two highly specific articles targeting search terms your buyer uses. Free traffic that compounds over time.

Pick one or two platforms. Don't try all of them at once.

---

## 4. Run a Small, Targeted Paid Ad Test

Once you have a few sales and know who bought, you can test paid traffic with as little as $5–$10/day.

- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads:** Best for physical products and lifestyle goods. Use a broad interest target and let the algorithm learn.
- **Google Shopping ads:** Best if people are actively searching for what you sell.
- **TikTok ads:** High reach, lower cost-per-click right now, good for younger demographics.

Don't spend big until you know what message converts. Test one ad with a clear offer.

---

## 5. Reach Out to Micro-Influencers

Micro-influencers (1,000–50,000 followers) in your niche are often open to free product in exchange for a post.

- Search Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube for creators who already talk about your product category.
- Send a short, direct DM: who you are, what you sell, what you're offering (free product), what you'd like in return (an honest post or story).
- Expect a low response rate — message 20 to get 3 responses.

One authentic post from a trusted creator can bring more sales than weeks of organic posting.

---

## 6. Make It Easy to Share and Reward Referrals

Once you have your first few customers, turn them into ambassadors.

- Ask for reviews and testimonials immediately after purchase. Use them everywhere.
- Offer a referral discount: "Give 10% off to a friend, get 10% off your next order."
- Include a handwritten note or small extra in packaging — people post this stuff.

---

## 7. List on Marketplaces or Aggregators (Where Relevant)

If you're not there already:
- **Etsy** (handmade, vintage, craft)
- **Amazon Handmade** or standard seller
- **Google Shopping** (free product listings are available)
- **Local Facebook Marketplace** for physical goods

Marketplaces have existing buyers. Use them to build early traction while your own store gains SEO authority.

---

## Quick Priority Order for This Week

1. Message 20 people in your network today.
2. Find 2–3 online communities where your buyer lives. Join and post thoughtfully.
3. Post 3 short videos showing your product in use.
4. DM 10 micro-influencers with a free product offer.

You don't need all 100 customers at once. Focus on getting the first 10. The next 90 come easier once you have proof of concept, real reviews, and a clearer sense of who your best customer is.
