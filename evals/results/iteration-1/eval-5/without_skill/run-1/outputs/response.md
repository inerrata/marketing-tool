# Defining Your Brand Voice

To define your brand voice, I need to understand a few things about your business. Since you haven't shared details yet, I'll guide you through the process with questions AND provide a framework so you can build it yourself.

---

## Step 1: Answer These Foundational Questions

Work through these to clarify your brand's identity:

**About Your Business**
1. What does your company do, and who do you serve?
2. What problem do you solve for your customers?
3. What makes you different from competitors?

**About Your Audience**
4. Who is your ideal customer? (Age, profession, values, lifestyle)
5. How does your customer talk? What words or phrases do they use?
6. What does your customer care most about when choosing a brand like yours?

**About Your Personality**
7. If your brand were a person, how would you describe them? (e.g., "a trusted mentor," "a witty best friend," "a no-nonsense expert")
8. List 3–5 adjectives that describe how you want to sound. (e.g., warm, bold, authoritative, playful, direct)
9. List 3–5 adjectives that describe how you do NOT want to sound. (e.g., corporate, aggressive, fluffy, formal)

**About Your Content**
10. What channels do you publish on? (Website, email, social, ads, etc.)
11. Do you have any existing content you feel proud of? What makes it work?

---

## Step 2: The Brand Voice Framework

Once you answer those questions, use this structure to document your voice:

### Brand Voice Statement
A one- or two-sentence summary:
> *"[Brand name] sounds like [personality descriptor] — [what that means in practice]. We are [tone trait 1] and [tone trait 2], never [what you avoid]."*

**Example:**
> "Acme sounds like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be an expert. We are clear and encouraging, never condescending or jargon-heavy."

---

### Voice Pillars (3–4 core traits)

For each trait, define:
- **The trait** (one word or short phrase)
- **What it means** (2–3 sentences)
- **In practice** (a "we do / we don't" table)

**Template:**

| Trait | Description | We Do | We Don't |
|-------|-------------|-------|----------|
| **[Trait 1]** | What this means for how we write | Specific behavior | What to avoid |
| **[Trait 2]** | | | |
| **[Trait 3]** | | | |

**Example — if your brand is a modern wellness company:**

| Trait | Description | We Do | We Don't |
|-------|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Empowering** | We believe customers are capable; we give them tools, not orders. | Use "you can," offer choices, celebrate progress | Use "you must," shame-based language, overload with warnings |
| **Warm** | We speak like a caring friend, not a clinical provider. | Use contractions, first names, conversational phrasing | Use cold medical jargon, passive voice, corporate-speak |
| **Direct** | We respect our audience's time. | Lead with the point, use short sentences | Bury the lede, over-qualify, use filler phrases |

---

### Tone Variations by Context

Voice is consistent; tone adapts to context. Map your tone shifts:

| Context | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| Social media | More playful, casual | "This one's for the 11pm overthinkers." |
| Website homepage | Confident, inspiring | "Built for people who don't settle." |
| Customer support | Warmer, patient, empathetic | "We totally understand — here's how we'll fix it." |
| Sales/ads | Direct, benefit-forward | "Cut your prep time in half. Here's how." |
| Error messages | Light, reassuring | "Oops! That link expired. Let's get you back on track." |

---

### Word Lists

**Use freely:**
(Words that feel on-brand)
_e.g., "straightforward," "real," "built," "you," "together," "results"_

**Use sparingly:**
(Words that can work but need care)
_e.g., "innovative," "seamless," "world-class"_

**Avoid:**
(Words that feel off-brand or overused in your industry)
_e.g., "synergy," "leverage," "disrupt," "guru," "crushing it"_

---

## Step 3: Test Your Voice

Run any piece of content through these questions:
- Does this sound like a real person talking, or a press release?
- Would my ideal customer relate to this?
- Does it match our voice pillars?
- Would I be comfortable if a competitor said the exact same thing? (If yes, it's probably too generic.)
