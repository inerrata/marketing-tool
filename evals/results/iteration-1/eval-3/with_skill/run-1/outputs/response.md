Assuming: mid-range/premium coffee subscription, B2C audience, primary goal is to drive clicks and purchases, offer is a Black Friday discount (specific % unknown, so kept flexible). Adjust if wrong.

---

Here are 3 Black Friday subject line options, each taking a distinct angle:

**1. Urgency / Scarcity**
`Your Black Friday beans won't last — grab them now`

*Why it works:* Ties the product directly to the event, uses "won't last" to signal real scarcity, and the imperative "grab" drives action without resorting to all-caps or excessive punctuation.

---

**2. Direct Benefit (Price-led)**
`Black Friday: 30% off your next 3 months of coffee`

*Why it works:* Leads with the concrete offer upfront. Subscribers scanning a full inbox want to know the deal immediately — no guessing, no click required to evaluate whether it's worth opening. Adjust the percentage to your actual offer.

---

**3. Curiosity / Identity**
`The one Black Friday deal a coffee snob actually needs`

*Why it works:* Speaks directly to the subscriber's self-image (they didn't sign up for generic coffee), uses mild irreverence to stand out in a sea of shouty BF emails, and the word "actually" does light work to set your brand apart from commodity discounts.

---

**Quick recommendation:** Option 2 tends to win on open rate in discount-heavy send windows like Black Friday because it gives readers a reason before they open. Option 3 is strongest if your brand has a distinct voice and a loyal, identity-driven subscriber base. A/B test 2 vs. 3 if volume allows.
