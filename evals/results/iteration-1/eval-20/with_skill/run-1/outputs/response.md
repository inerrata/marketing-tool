# Is Your $400 CAC Healthy?

**Short answer: Yes — your unit economics are solid, but not exceptional. Here's the full picture.**

---

## The Key Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | $400 |
| Monthly Revenue per Customer (MRR) | $50 |
| Average Customer Lifetime | 18 months |
| Lifetime Value (LTV) | $900 (18 × $50) |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | **2.25x** |
| CAC Payback Period | **8 months** |

---

## How to Interpret These

### LTV:CAC Ratio — 2.25x

The widely used benchmark for subscription businesses:

- **Below 1x**: You're losing money on every customer. Unsustainable.
- **1x–3x**: Viable but thin. You're covering acquisition costs but margins are limited.
- **3x**: The classic "healthy" SaaS benchmark.
- **Above 3x**: Strong — either your CAC is low, retention is high, or both.

At **2.25x**, you're in the viable zone, but below the 3x benchmark most investors and operators target. It's not a red flag, but it's a signal to either grow LTV (raise prices, reduce churn, upsell) or reduce CAC (improve conversion rates, shift to lower-cost channels).

### CAC Payback Period — 8 months

This tells you how long it takes to recoup what you spent acquiring each customer.

- **Under 12 months**: Generally healthy for subscription businesses.
- **12–18 months**: Acceptable but means you're tying up significant cash.
- **Over 18 months**: Risky — customers may churn before you break even.

At **8 months**, your payback period is good. You recover your acquisition spend well before the average customer churns, giving you a real profit window of roughly 10 months per customer.

---

## What Would Make This Stronger

1. **Improve retention** — if you could stretch average lifetime from 18 to 24 months, LTV jumps to $1,200 and your LTV:CAC ratio hits 3x. Retention improvements compound faster than acquisition improvements.

2. **Add an upsell or expansion path** — a second tier at $75–100/month, or an annual plan discount, raises LTV without touching CAC.

3. **Reduce CAC below $300** — at $300 CAC with the same LTV of $900, you hit a 3x ratio. Look at which acquisition channels have the lowest CAC and double down there.

---

## Bottom Line

Your $400 CAC is **reasonable and profitable**, not broken. You're recovering costs in 8 months and earning a positive return over the customer lifetime. The business is viable. To reach best-in-class efficiency, the lever with the highest impact is **retention** — keeping customers an average of 6 more months transforms your unit economics meaningfully.
