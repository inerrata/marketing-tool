Happy to design your welcome sequence — before I write it, three quick questions so it's sharp rather than generic:

1. **Who is this for?** Describe your ideal subscriber as specifically as you can — their role, situation, and what problem or goal brought them to your newsletter. (e.g. "early-stage founders trying to hire their first engineers," not just "entrepreneurs")

2. **What's the one thing you want subscribers to do or believe after the sequence ends?** Pick one: visit your paid product, reply to you, forward the newsletter, upgrade to a paid tier, book a call, just stay subscribed and engaged. The whole sequence should funnel toward that single outcome.

3. **What is the newsletter about, and what's the single most valuable thing a new subscriber gets from it?** Not the topic in one word — give me the specific promise: what will they know, be able to do, or feel after reading regularly that they couldn't before?

Bonus if relevant: How long have you been publishing, do you have any testimonials or open-rate data worth referencing, and are there any tone constraints (formal/casual, brand voice)?

Once I have those answers I'll write the full sequence — subject lines, preview text, and body copy for each email, mapped to a recommended send schedule.
