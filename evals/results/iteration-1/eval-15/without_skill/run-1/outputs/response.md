# Newsletter Welcome Sequence

A strong welcome sequence turns new subscribers into engaged readers and future customers. Here is a 5-email sequence you can deploy immediately, with suggested send timing, subject lines, and copy frameworks.

---

## Overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 — The Warm Welcome | Immediately on signup | Deliver on your promise, set expectations |
| 2 — Your Story | Day 2 | Build personal connection |
| 3 — Best of the Archive | Day 4 | Demonstrate value, reduce unsubscribes |
| 4 — What I Believe | Day 7 | Share your worldview, attract true fans |
| 5 — The Invitation | Day 10 | Drive next action (reply, purchase, follow) |

---

## Email 1 — The Warm Welcome
**Send:** Immediately after signup  
**Subject line options:**
- "You're in — here's what happens next"
- "Welcome. Let's start here."
- "Your first issue starts now"

**Body framework:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> You made it. Welcome to [Newsletter Name].
>
> Every [cadence: week / Tuesday / month], I send [brief description of what you cover — e.g., "one practical idea for building a profitable solo business"].
>
> Here's what you can expect:
> - [Benefit 1 — e.g., "Actionable frameworks, not fluffy theory"]
> - [Benefit 2 — e.g., "Real examples from real businesses"]
> - [Benefit 3 — e.g., "Short enough to read over coffee"]
>
> **Your first step:** To make sure my emails land in your inbox (not spam), reply to this email with "Got it" — or just say hi. I read every reply.
>
> Talk soon,  
> [Your Name]
>
> P.S. [Optional freebie delivery line, or a teaser for Email 2: "Tomorrow I'll share why I started this newsletter — and the mistake that almost made me quit."]

---

## Email 2 — Your Story
**Send:** Day 2  
**Subject line options:**
- "Why I almost quit (before I started)"
- "The honest reason I write this newsletter"
- "Here's what I was doing three years ago…"

**Body framework:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> Before you read another issue, I want you to know who's writing it.
>
> [2–3 sentences: Where were you before? What problem were you facing or what journey were you on?]
>
> [2–3 sentences: What changed? What did you figure out, build, or discover?]
>
> [1–2 sentences: Why does this newsletter exist — what transformation do you want for your readers?]
>
> I don't write this newsletter to be another voice in the noise. I write it because [your genuine reason].
>
> If that resonates, you're in the right place.
>
> — [Your Name]

**Tips:** Keep this personal and specific. Real details (a year, a number, a city, a failure) build more trust than vague inspiration.

## Email 3 — Best of the Archive
**Send:** Day 4  
**Subject line options:**
- "Start here: my 3 most-read pieces"
- "In case you missed it — my best work"
- "The 3 emails readers always forward"

**Body framework:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> You've only been here a few days, but I've been writing [Newsletter Name] for [X months/years]. That means there's a lot of good stuff you haven't seen yet.
>
> Here are three pieces I'd suggest starting with:
>
> **1. [Title of popular/foundational piece]**  
> [One sentence on why it matters or what the reader will take away]  
> → [Link]
>
> **2. [Title of popular/foundational piece]**  
> [One sentence on why it matters or what the reader will take away]  
> → [Link]
>
> **3. [Title of popular/foundational piece]**  
> [One sentence on why it matters or what the reader will take away]  
> → [Link]
>
> Any of these resonate? Hit reply and let me know — I'll point you toward what to read next.
>
> — [Your Name]

**Tips:** If your newsletter is brand new, use this email to link to relevant content you've published elsewhere (a blog, LinkedIn, podcast), or replace it with a "frequently asked questions" format.

## Email 4 — What I Believe
**Send:** Day 7  
**Subject line options:**
- "An unpopular opinion about [your topic]"
- "The thing most [audience] get wrong"
- "I disagree with almost everyone on this"

**Body framework:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> Here's something I believe that a lot of people in [your space] won't say out loud:
>
> [State a clear, specific point of view — a belief, a contrarian take, or a principle you stand behind.]
>
> [Explain why you believe it — 2–4 short paragraphs with evidence, examples, or personal experience.]
>
> [Acknowledge the counterargument briefly, then restate your position.]
>
> I'm not right about everything. But I do think this matters — and it shapes everything I write here.
>
> Do you agree? Disagree? I genuinely want to know.
>
> — [Your Name]

**Tips:** This email is designed to filter in your best-fit readers and filter out poor fits — both are good outcomes. Don't be afraid to take a real stance.

## Email 5 — The Invitation
**Send:** Day 10  
**Subject line options:**
- "One small ask"
- "What happens next"
- "Ready for the next step?"

**Body framework:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> You've been here for about a week and a half. I hope the last few emails have been worth your time.
>
> I want to make you an offer / share something with you:
>
> [Choose ONE clear call to action. Examples below:]
>
> **Option A — Community/Engagement:**
> "Hit reply and tell me: what's the #1 challenge you're dealing with right now in [topic area]? I read every reply and often turn your questions into future issues."
>
> **Option B — Product/Service:**
> "If you're ready to go deeper, I offer [product/service]. Here's what it is and who it's for: [link]"
>
> **Option C — Social Follow:**
> "If you want more between issues, I share [type of content] on [platform] — [link]. Come say hi."
>
> **Option D — Referral:**
> "If you've found this useful, I'd love it if you forwarded this email to one friend who'd benefit. It's the best way to help me grow."
>
> Either way — thanks for being here. More good stuff is on its way.
>
> — [Your Name]

## Additional Tips for the Sequence

**Tone:** Write the way you talk. These emails should sound like a real person, not a brand.

**Length:** Keep each email under 400 words where possible. Respect your readers' time.

**Deliverability:** Ask for a reply in Email 1. Gmail and other clients rank senders higher when subscribers reply — this keeps you out of Promotions.

**Segmentation:** If your email platform supports it, tag subscribers who click certain links and use those tags to send them more relevant follow-up.

**Testing:** A/B test subject lines in your first 500 subscribers. The difference between a 25% and 45% open rate is usually the subject line alone.

**Unsubscribes:** Expect 5–15% unsubscribes during a welcome sequence. This is healthy — it cleans your list and improves deliverability for everyone who stays.

## Questions to Customize This Sequence

If you'd like a more tailored version, here's what would help me refine it further:

1. What is the newsletter about — what topic or niche does it cover?
2. Who is your target reader (job title, life stage, problem they're trying to solve)?
3. What is your publishing cadence (daily, weekly, monthly)?
4. Do you have a product, service, or community you'd like to promote?
5. What platform are you using (Beehiiv, Substack, Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)?
6. Is this a new newsletter or an existing one with an archive?

With those answers, I can rewrite any of these emails with your specific voice, audience, and offer.
