Before I write this positioning statement, three quick questions so it's sharp rather than generic:

1. **Who exactly is your target agency?** The narrower the better — e.g., "boutique creative agencies with 5–20 people," "performance-marketing agencies managing 50+ client campaigns," or "full-service digital agencies billing on retainer." The smaller and more specific the slice, the stronger the positioning.

2. **What is the one thing your tool does that the main alternatives don't?** This could be a workflow feature, an integration, a pricing model, a collaboration approach — anything you'd point to if a prospect said "why not just use Asana / Monday / ClickUp?"

3. **Who or what are you really competing against?** A specific rival tool (and which one)? Agencies cobbling together spreadsheets + email? An internal ops process they built themselves? The main alternative shapes the "unlike X, we…" half of the statement — without knowing it, the differentiator can't be positioned against anything real.

---

Once you answer these, I'll produce a tight positioning statement using the format:

> *For [target agency] who [pain or need], [product] is the [category] that [key differentiated benefit], because [reason to believe]. Unlike [main alternative], [product] [key difference].*

I'll also flag which parts need proof to be defensible, and offer a shorter value-proposition variant you can drop into a homepage hero or pitch deck.
