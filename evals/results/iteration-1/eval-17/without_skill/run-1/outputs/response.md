## General Pricing Page Audit: The Most Common Conversion Killers

### 1. Value Proposition Clarity
**What to check:** Does the headline on the pricing page communicate what the product does and why it's worth paying for — or does it just say "Pricing"?

**Common failure:** The page assumes the visitor already understands the product's value. They often don't.

**Fix:** Lead with a value-anchoring headline like *"Everything you need to [achieve outcome] — starting at $X/mo"* rather than just a plan grid.

---

### 2. Too Many or Too Few Plans
**What to check:** How many tiers do you have?

**Common failure:**
- 2 plans: Visitors can't find a middle ground and default to the cheaper option or leave.
- 4+ plans: Choice paralysis. Visitors can't decide and leave.

**Fix:** 3 plans is the proven sweet spot. Use the middle plan as the anchor (highlight it with "Most Popular" or a visual treatment).

---

### 3. No Recommended Plan
**What to check:** Is one plan visually highlighted or labeled as recommended?

**Common failure:** All plans look equal. The visitor has no default to anchor on.

**Fix:** Highlight the plan you most want people to choose. Use a colored border, badge ("Best Value"), or slightly larger card.

---

### 4. Feature Comparison Confusion
**What to check:** Are the features listed in a way that maps to real customer outcomes, or is it a raw feature dump?

**Common failure:** Lines like "Up to 500 API calls/mo" or "Advanced analytics" mean nothing to a prospect who hasn't used the product.

**Fix:** Translate features into outcomes. Instead of "Advanced analytics," write "See exactly where users drop off so you can fix it." Add tooltips for technical terms.

---

### 5. Missing or Buried Social Proof
**What to check:** Is there any social proof on the pricing page itself (not just the homepage)?

**Common failure:** Testimonials and logos live on the homepage but disappear by the time the visitor reaches pricing — right when trust is most needed.

**Fix:** Add 2–3 short testimonials focused specifically on value/ROI near the plan cards. A line like *"We switched from [competitor] and saved $400/mo while getting better results."* is gold.

---

### 6. Weak or Unclear CTAs
**What to check:** What do the CTA buttons say?

**Common failure:** Generic text like "Get Started" or "Sign Up" creates friction because the visitor doesn't know what happens next.

**Fix:** Make CTAs specific and low-risk. Examples:
- "Start Free 14-Day Trial — No Credit Card"
- "Try the Pro Plan Free"
- "Talk to Sales (Response in 1 Business Day)"

---

### 7. No Risk Reversal
**What to check:** Is there a money-back guarantee, free trial, or cancellation policy stated clearly?

**Common failure:** The visitor is being asked to commit money with no stated safety net. This is the single biggest source of silent drop-off on pricing pages.

**Fix:** Put a short risk-reversal statement directly under or near the CTA buttons: *"30-day money-back guarantee. Cancel anytime."*

---

### 8. Annual vs. Monthly Toggle Hidden or Misleading
**What to check:** If you have annual/monthly pricing, is the toggle prominent? Is the savings clearly quantified?

**Common failure:** The toggle is hard to find, or it just says "Annual" without communicating the savings amount.

**Fix:** Default to showing annual pricing (you want annual revenue) and display the savings explicitly: *"Annual — Save 20%"* with the monthly-equivalent price shown.

---

### 9. No FAQ Section
**What to check:** Is there an FAQ below the plan cards?

**Common failure:** Visitors have objections and questions (Can I change plans? What happens when I hit my limit? Do you offer discounts for nonprofits?) that go unanswered. They leave instead of asking.

**Fix:** Add 6–10 FAQ items addressing the most common objections. "What if I need more than the plan allows?" and "Is there a free trial?" are almost always needed.

---

### 10. Page Speed and Mobile Experience
**What to check:** Load time on mobile. Readability of the plan table on small screens.

**Common failure:** Plan comparison tables collapse badly on mobile, making it impossible to compare options.

**Fix:** On mobile, stack plans vertically as individual cards. Ensure the CTA button is always visible without scrolling. Check Google PageSpeed Insights for load time — aim for under 3 seconds.

---

## Quick-Win Priority Order

If you can only fix 3 things first, fix these:

1. **Add a risk-reversal statement** (money-back guarantee or free trial) directly under your CTAs.
2. **Highlight a recommended plan** with a visual callout.
3. **Add social proof** (1–2 testimonials) directly on the pricing page.

These three changes address the most common reasons prospects stall at pricing: fear of commitment, decision paralysis, and lack of trust.
