# Win-Back Email Sequence: 60-Day Inactive Users

**Assumptions:** SaaS or app product with a logged-in experience; "customers" means paid or registered users who have gone dormant; no specific brand voice specified, so tone is warm and direct; sequences are triggered automatically at the day marks noted. Adjust if wrong.

---

## Sequence Overview

| # | Trigger (days since last login) | Subject Line | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 60 | We miss you — here's what's new | Re-engage with curiosity + value |
| 2 | Day 67 | The one thing most [role] forget to do | Surface a concrete use case |
| 3 | Day 74 | Your account is collecting dust | Create urgency with a loss frame |
| 4 | Day 81 | A personal offer, just for you | Incentive + last-chance |
| 5 | Day 90 | Should we say goodbye? | Final re-permission / close the loop |

---

## Email 1 — Day 60
**Goal:** Reopen the relationship. Low pressure. Focus on what they're missing, not what they did wrong.

**Subject:** We miss you — here's what's new
**Preview text:** Three things that changed since your last visit.

---

Hi [First name],

It's been a while since you last logged in — 60 days, to be exact.

We're not here to guilt-trip you. We just wanted to share a few things that landed since you were last around:

- **[Feature A]** — [one-line outcome, e.g., "cuts your weekly report setup from 20 minutes to 2"]
- **[Feature B]** — [one-line outcome]
- **[Improvement C]** — [one-line outcome]

If any of those sound useful, your account is exactly where you left it.

[**Log back in →**]

— [Team / Sender name]

---

## Email 2 — Day 67
**Goal:** Deliver standalone value (a tip, insight, or reminder) tied to the product's core job-to-be-done. Reminds them why they signed up without being promotional.

**Subject:** The one thing most [role/persona, e.g., "freelancers"] forget to do in [relevant workflow]
**Preview text:** Takes 5 minutes. Saves hours.

---

Hi [First name],

Quick one.

Most [role] skip [specific high-value action the product enables], usually because it feels like extra work upfront.

It isn't. Here's what it looks like in practice:

1. [Step 1 — concrete, tied to product]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

The result: [specific outcome, e.g., "clients stop chasing you for updates; you stop re-explaining the same thing every week"].

You can do this in [product] in about 5 minutes. Your account is still live.

[**Try it now →**]

— [Sender]

*P.S. — Replying to this email reaches a real person. If something pushed you away, we'd genuinely like to know.*

---

## Email 3 — Day 74
**Goal:** Shift from nurture to mild urgency. Use a loss frame (what inaction costs) rather than a benefits recap.

**Subject:** Your account is collecting dust
**Preview text:** That's not a complaint — here's why it matters.

---

Hi [First name],

Dormant accounts have a cost you might not see: [specific consequence tied to your product, e.g., "your integrations stop syncing," "your saved templates go stale," "your team loses the shared history"].

We're not saying that to scare you. We're saying it because the fix takes about two minutes, and the alternative — starting over from scratch — takes considerably longer.

Here's where you left off: [**[last meaningful action / project name / saved state] →**]

Pick it back up whenever you're ready. We'll be here.

— [Sender]

---

## Email 4 — Day 81
**Goal:** Introduce a concrete incentive. Make it feel personal, not mass-blast.

**Subject:** A personal offer, just for you
**Preview text:** We don't do this often. Here's why we're doing it now.

---

Hi [First name],

We don't send discount codes to everyone — but we do send them to people we genuinely want back.

You're one of them.

**[Offer: e.g., 30% off your next month / one free month / free upgrade to Pro for 30 days]**

Use code **[CODE]** at checkout. It's valid for the next 7 days.

[**Claim your offer →**]

No pressure. If timing isn't right, I get it. But if cost or uncertainty is what's kept you away — this is a good moment.

— [Name, with a title like "Head of Customer Experience" to signal this isn't automated]

*Offer expires [date].*

---

## Email 5 — Day 90
**Goal:** Re-permission / close the loop. Either win them back or let them opt out cleanly. This preserves list health and deliverability.

**Subject:** Should we say goodbye?
**Preview text:** We'll follow your lead.

---

Hi [First name],

It's been 90 days since you last logged in. We've tried to stay in touch — shared what's new, offered a discount, sent a tip or two.

If [product] isn't a fit right now, that's completely okay. Situations change.

Two options:

**[Stay in touch]** — We'll keep you on our list, but we'll back off on the check-ins. You'll only hear from us for important product updates.

**[Remove me from all emails]** — No hard feelings. You can always come back at [product URL].

Either way, thanks for giving [product] a shot. We hope to see you again.

— [Founder / Customer Success lead name]

## Sequence Notes

**Timing rationale:** Emails are spaced 7 days apart after the initial trigger to maintain presence without harassment. The final email at day 90 acts as a hard re-permission checkpoint — anyone who clicks neither link within 14 days should be suppressed from marketing sends (not deleted; just moved to a minimal-contact segment).

**Personalization to layer in (in priority order):**
1. First name (table stakes)
2. Last action / last project / last feature used — makes Email 3's "where you left off" link concrete
3. Plan type — the offer in Email 4 should vary by tier (no point offering a discount to someone on a free plan)
4. Industry/role — subject lines in Emails 1 and 2 sharpen significantly with this

**What to measure:**
- Open rate by email (flag if Email 1 open rate is below 15% — deliverability or subject-line problem)
- Click-to-login rate (primary KPI per email)
- Re-activation rate = users who log in within 7 days of opening any email in the sequence
- Unsubscribe spike on Email 3 or 4 signals the tone is off; adjust loss-frame intensity

**Variants worth A/B testing first:**
- Email 1 subject: "We miss you" vs. "[First name], your [Product] is waiting"
- Email 4 offer: discount vs. free upgrade vs. extended trial
- Email 5 framing: "Should we say goodbye?" vs. "One last thing before we go quiet"
