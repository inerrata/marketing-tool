# What to Post on LinkedIn for Your Consulting Business

LinkedIn is the single best organic channel for consultants. Here's a practical framework for what to post and how often.

---

## The Core Goal

Every post should do at least one of these things:
- Demonstrate your expertise
- Build trust with your ideal client
- Make your point of view memorable
- Attract inbound leads or referrals

---

## Content Categories That Work for Consultants

### 1. Lessons from Client Work (anonymized)
Share a specific problem you solved, what you learned, and what others can take away. This is your strongest content — it proves you do the work, not just talk about it.

> *Example: "A client came to me with a pricing problem. They thought it was a positioning problem. Here's how we figured out the real issue — and fixed it in 6 weeks."*

### 2. Contrarian or Nuanced Takes
Share a view that pushes back on conventional wisdom in your industry. This positions you as a thinker, not just a practitioner.

> *Example: "Everyone says to niching down is the answer. Sometimes it's the wrong move. Here's when it backfires."*

### 3. Frameworks and Mental Models
Give away a structured way of thinking about a problem your clients face. This is incredibly shareable and positions you as a go-to expert.

> *Example: A 2x2 matrix, a 3-step process, a checklist — anything that simplifies complexity.*

### 4. Behind-the-Scenes of Your Work
Show how you think, how you prepare for client calls, how you structure proposals, what your actual workflow looks like. People buy people — this builds familiarity.

### 5. Client Results (with permission)
Specific, outcome-driven stories. Not vague ("helped a client grow") but concrete ("helped a 12-person team cut onboarding time from 6 weeks to 2").

### 6. Questions That Spark Discussion
Ask your target audience what they're struggling with, what they've tried, what they think about a trend. This drives comments and surfaces potential leads.

> *Example: "What's the most common mistake you see in [your industry] when it comes to [your specialty]?"*

### 7. Personal Story with a Professional Lesson
A moment of failure, an unexpected insight, a pivot you made — tied back to something your audience can apply. Humanizes you without oversharing.

---

## Posting Cadence

- **Minimum:** 2–3 posts per week
- **Sweet spot:** 4–5 posts per week
- **Consistency beats volume** — better to post 3 times reliably than 10 times one week and zero the next

---

## Format Tips

- **Short posts (3–7 lines)** get high engagement — use white space, avoid walls of text
- **Lead with a strong first line** — it's the only thing visible before "see more"
- **No link in the post body** — LinkedIn suppresses reach for posts with outbound links; put links in the first comment
- **Use personal photos** occasionally — they consistently outperform stock images
- **Native carousels (PDFs)** are excellent for frameworks and step-by-step content

---

## What to Avoid

- Pure self-promotion with no value ("Excited to announce we now offer X service!")
- Generic motivational content unrelated to your niche
- Reposting articles without your own commentary
- Posting and disappearing — spend 20–30 minutes engaging with comments after you post

3. **What stage is your business?** (just starting, established but want more leads, looking to raise rates)
