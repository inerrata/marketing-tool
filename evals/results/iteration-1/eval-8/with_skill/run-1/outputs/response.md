Great question — LinkedIn can be a real client-generation engine for a consulting business, but the answer depends heavily on what you're selling and who you're trying to reach. Before I give you a content plan that's actually useful rather than generic, three quick questions:

1. **Who exactly are you trying to reach?** The more specific the better — e.g. "HR directors at mid-size manufacturing companies" vs. "business owners." This shapes everything from the topics you cover to the tone you use.

2. **What's the one thing you want LinkedIn to do for your business?** For example: generate inbound inquiries, stay top-of-mind with existing contacts, build credibility with a new vertical, or grow your network toward a specific goal. (Most consultants try to do all of these at once and end up doing none well.)

3. **What problem do you solve, and who is your main competition — another consultant, an internal hire, or just clients doing nothing?** This tells me what angle to take with your content so it positions you distinctly, not just as "another consultant."

Once I have those, I can give you a specific content mix (what types of posts to write, at what cadence, on what topics) that's built around your business rather than a recycled template.
